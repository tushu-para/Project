Socialism in India

Socialism in India has its roots in the country's struggle for independence and the vision of its founding leaders, like Jawaharlal Nehru. It emphasizes a mixed economy where both the public and private sectors coexist, with a strong emphasis on social justice, welfare, and equitable distribution of resources. After independence in 1947, India adopted socialist principles to guide its economic policies, focusing on land reforms, nationalization of key industries, and state-led development initiatives to reduce poverty and inequality. The influence of socialism can still be seen in India's policies today, though economic liberalization since the 1990s has shifted the balance towards a more market-oriented approach. Socialism in India represents an effort to create a society where economic progress benefits all sections, especially the marginalized.
